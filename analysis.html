01. RESOLUTION GAP

WHY A CONFLICT ONTOLOGY IS NECESSARY

STRUCTURAL PROBLEM

Modern institutions are flooded with contentious communication: email chains, board minutes, media cycles, social feeds, legal memos. The volume of conflictual information grows exponentially; the capacity of humans to track its structure does not. That mismatch creates the Resolution Gap: we detect more friction than we can systematically resolve.

Tacitus is built to address that gap. It treats every conflict or pre-conflict situation as a system of interacting actors, interests, constraints, and trajectories of polarization. The goal is not to predict history, but to maintain a continuously updated, structural understanding of where escalation is likely, where concessions are possible, and where common ground still exists.

Continuous Understanding LIVE MODEL

Tacitus keeps a living map of conflicts and pre-conflict tensions, updating as new information arrives. No more frozen PowerPoint snapshots of yesterday’s crisis.

Automated Monitoring EARLY-WARNING

The ontology powers continuous monitoring of polarization trajectories, veto paths, and breakdown risks. The system surfaces where attention and mediation capacity are most needed.

Resolution Technologies ACTIONABLE

The same structural model drives scenario testing, common-ground proposals, and negotiation scripts tailored to the mandates and red lines of each actor.

02. TACITUS ONTOLOGY

STRUCTURAL RULES OF ENGAGEMENT // v3.3

ONTOLOGY STABLE

>> CIRCUITS OF POWER, NOT HEROES AND VILLAINS

Tacitus rejects the idea that outcomes are mainly driven by personalities or intentions. Instead, it adopts a structural realism: outcomes emerge from the geometry of constraints in which actors operate.

Every conflict system is modeled as a circuit between:

  • > Incumbents: those currently holding the centre of power or agenda-setting capacity.
  • > Challengers: actors seeking to shift the status quo, openly or quietly.
  • > Veto Players: actors whose assent is necessary to change course.
  • > Selectorates: constituencies whose support confers or withdraws legitimacy.

The ontology encodes how these roles interact, which coalitions are viable, and where Win-Sets (overlapping acceptable outcomes) may exist. When Win-Sets shrink or vanish, Tacitus flags a transition from negotiable disagreement toward pre-conflict.

S / W

Selectorate & Survival

Distinguishes between who could, in theory, influence leadership (selectorate), and the smaller coalition that must be kept inside the tent. When that coalition feels existentially threatened, compromise space narrows rapidly.

V

Veto Players

Any actor whose consent is required to move away from the status quo. Tacitus tracks how many veto players exist, how far apart they are, and how cohesive their internal politics are.

K / D

Kinetic & Discursive Planes

Power operates through bodies and assets (kinetic) and through narrative and legitimacy (discursive). The ontology encodes how failures on one plane reprice risk on the other.

Ω

Win-Set Geometry

The space of outcomes each veto player could live with. Tacitus maps when that space grows (ripeness) or collapses (hardening), and what guarantees might re-open it.

03. METRICS LAYER

POWER, RISK & POLARIZATION TRAJECTORIES

METRICS ONLINE
Power Metric NODE MASS

Structural capacity to force or block change: legal authority, control of chokepoints, agenda-setting power, or capacity to impose costs on others.

Risk Metric GLOW / HEAT

Exposure to adverse outcomes: reputational collapse, sanctions, loss of mandate, economic shocks, or internal splits that could cascade across the graph.

Coherence Metric INTERNAL FRICTION

Measures how internally aligned a node or coalition is. Fragmented actors speak with many voices, weakening commitments and making agreements fragile.

Trajectory Metric POLARIZATION ARC

Tracks whether disagreements are stabilizing, oscillating, or hardening into identity-bound conflict. This is the early-warning layer for pre-conflict situations.

04. VECTOR ANATOMY

HOW POWER FLOWS THROUGH THE GRAPH

EDGE TYPES

>> HARD POWER VECTORS

  • VETO — Legal or procedural kill switch: votes, rulings, security-council-style vetoes, binding shareholder thresholds.
  • CONFLICT — Strikes, blockades, lawsuits, kinetic force: moments where bargaining collapses into damage.
  • FUNDING — Control of capital flows, budgets, grants: whoever funds can reprice which strategies are viable.
  • DEPENDENCY — Structural reliance on energy, logistics, supply chains, key personnel, or data platforms.
  • GUARANTEE — Security, political, or economic guarantees that make agreements credible over time.

>> SOFT POWER VECTORS

  • NARRATIVE — Frames, stories, leaks, and symbol-work that confer or strip legitimacy.
  • PRESSURE — Protest, boycotts, campaigns, naming-and-shaming: raising the cost of inaction.
  • ALLIANCE — Formal or informal pacts aggregating power, often creating “tripwires” for escalation.
  • PROXY — Indirect engagement via intermediaries to lower visible risk while still shaping outcomes.
  • MEDIATION CHANNEL — Structured pathways where trust is sufficient for reframing and compromise exploration.

06. ONTOLOGY AGENT

DETERMINISTIC PIPELINE, EXPLAINABLE REASONING

AGENTIC ENGINE

At the core of Tacitus is the Ontology Agent: an AI-augmented reasoning system that takes unstructured, often emotional inputs and commits them to the ontology in a deterministic, auditable pipeline.

>> REASONING PIPELINE

  • 1. Structural Reading
    Identify who is involved, what they can actually do, what they fear losing, and what they must deliver to remain legitimate.
  • 2. Ontology Mapping
    Assign actors, interests, constraints, vectors, and metrics into the Tacitus schema so that different cases become comparable.
  • 3. Scenario Generation
    Generate plausible pathways: escalation, stalemate, partial settlement, or breakthrough under different combinations of guarantees and concessions.
  • 4. Resolution Support
    Translate structural insights into proposals, talking points, and negotiation strategies tailored to specific mandates.

>> BEYOND SEMANTIC SIMILARITY

Most AI systems stop at semantic similarity: clustering texts that “sound alike”. Tacitus treats that as a first step, not the destination.

  • > Similar cases inform structural priors, not copy-paste solutions.
  • > The Ontology Agent looks for relational archetypes: veto configurations, coalition shapes, polarization arcs.
  • > Conflict knowledge is stored as patterns of relations, not just documents, allowing agentic systems to reason over the graph of interactions, not merely over words.

The future of conflict intelligence is graph-aware and agentic: AI systems that understand who can move what, at what cost, and how structural changes ripple through an organization or a polity. Tacitus is designed explicitly for that future.

07. POLARIZATION & PRE-CONFLICT

TRACKING THE TRAJECTORIES BEFORE THEY BREAK

EARLY WARNING

Polarization is not just “people disagreeing strongly”. It becomes dangerous when disagreement solidifies into identity, when distrust spreads to procedures themselves, and when groups stop recognizing any shared authority capable of arbitrating disputes.

Tacitus treats polarization as a trajectory, not a label. The ontology and metrics layer allow us to observe how:

  • > coalitions sort into increasingly homogeneous camps,
  • > moderating actors lose centrality or credibility,
  • > narratives shift from “wrong on the issue” to “illegitimate as a partner”,
  • > vetoes are used less as safeguards and more as weapons.
Signal FROM CONTENT

Shifts in language, framing, and perceived harms signal where positions are hardening or where new grievances emerge.

Structure IN THE GRAPH

Tacitus tracks whether bridging actors still exist, whether channels of deliberation are open, and which actors can still “speak across” divides.

Intervention TARGETED

Early-warning is only useful if it suggests action: whom to bring into the room, which procedural guarantees to offer, which narratives to de-escalate first.

The Prism Lab builds on this ontology: it lets practitioners explore how different framings, concessions, or guarantees might change polarization arcs before conflicts cross critical thresholds.

08. TACITUS LAB

AI, DELIBERATION & THE FUTURE OF DEMOCRATIC DECISION-MAKING

RESEARCH MODE

Tacitus Lab is the experimental arm of the platform. It explores how AI-mediated deliberation and multi-agent systems can support better collective decisions without replacing human judgment.

Deliberative AI

Experiments inspired by deliberative democracy and Habermasian ideas: AI systems that summarize arguments fairly, surface overlapping proposals, and help groups see where they already agree more than they think.

Constitutional & Agentic Systems

Multi-agent architectures where different AI agents play distinct roles: advocate, critic, risk officer, mediator. Each is governed by explicit norms and constitutional rules rather than opaque prompts.

Subsidiarity & Freedom

The aim is not technocratic control, but subsidiarity: giving decisions back to the level closest to those affected, equipped with better structural intelligence. Protecting citizens’ freedom means making power legible, not automating it.

We are prototyping tools where institutions can:

  • > run “what-if” deliberations across different value frameworks,
  • > test whether proposed rules are robust under stress scenarios,
  • > identify when minority viewpoints are systematically ignored,
  • > generate agenda-setting questions that widen, rather than narrow, the conversation.

Tacitus Lab is where conflict ontology meets democratic theory: engineering systems that make it easier for free people and plural institutions to reach decisions they can live with — without surrendering moral agency to machines.
